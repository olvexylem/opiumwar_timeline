THE FIRST BATTLE OF THE FIRST OPIUM WAR BEGINS WHEN A BRITISH SHIP, THE ROYAL SAXON, ATTEMPTS TO SAIL TO CANTON.

3 NOV 1839:

  • BRITISH SHIP, ROYAL SAXON, APPROACHING CANTON WAS SIGNALED TO STOP AND TURN BACK.

  • A BRITISH SHIP IN THE BLOCKADE FIRED A WARNING SHOT.

  • THE CHINESE ADMIRAL STATIONED AT CANTON HEARD THE CANON SHOT FROM SHORE AND ORDERED HIS FLEET TO PROTECT THE ROYAL SAXON.

  • CONFUSED, THE COMMANDER OF THE BRITISH SHIP THAT ORGINALLY FIRED REQUESTED PERMISSION TO ENGAGE.

  • ANOTHER REQUEST TO ENGAGE WAS MADE. HOWEVER, INCOMING CHINESE WAR JUNKS BEARING RED FLAGS MADE THE BRITISH DECIDE TO ATTACK.

  • BRITISH NAVAL ARTILIERY OVERPOWERED THE CHINESE WAR JUNKS WHICH LATER RETREATED AND CONCLUDED THE FIRST REAL BATTLE OF THE OPIUM WARS.
