SECOND BATTLE OF CHUENPI

A RUMOR FROM AN OPIUM RUNNER PROMPTS ELLIOT TO PREEMPT AN ATTACK FROM THE CHINESE.

ON THE 7th of JANUARY 1841, ELLIOT COMMANDS BRITISH FORCES TO OPEN FIRE ON CHINESE FORtIFICATIONS AT CHUENPi, COMMENCING THE SECOND BATTLE OF CHUENPI.

×

THE SECOND BATTLE OF CHUENPI LASTED HOURS.

  • BRITISH GUNS CRUSHED CHINESE FORTIFICATIONS AND COUNTER BATTERIES.

  • INDIAN AND BRITISH MARINES LANDED ON SHORE AND PUSHED BACK CHINESE GROUND FORCES.

  • CHINESE BATTALIONS SUFFERED GREAT LOSSES.

BY 11:00AM, THE BRITISH FLAG FLEW OVER CHINESE BATTLEMENTS.CASUALTIES INCLUDE: CHINESE, 600 DEATHS, 100 CAPTURED. BRITISH, 30 INJURED.

THE NEXT MORNING, A CHINESE PHYSICIAN DELIVERED A FLAG OF TRUCE ON BEHALF OF QISHAN.

ON THE 20TH OF JANUARY 1841, ELLOIT MET WITH QISHAN ONCE MORE TO NEGOTIATE THE 'CONVENTION OF CHUENPI'.

THE CESSION OF THE ISLAND AND HARBOUR OF HONG KONG TO THE BRITISH CROWN.

ALL JUST CHARGES AND DUTIES TO THE EMPIRE [OF CHINA] UPON THE COMMERCE CARRIED ON THERE TO BE PAID AS IF THE TRADE WERE CONDUCTED AT WHAMPOA.

AN INDEMNITY TO THE BRITISH GOVERNMENT OF 6 MILLION DOLLARS.

DIRECT OFFICIAL INTERCOURSE BETWEEN THE COUNTRIES UPON EQUAL FOOTING.

THE TRADE OF THE PORT OF CANTON TO BE OPENED WITHIN 10 DAYS AFTER THE CHINESE NEW YEAR, AND TO BE CARRIED ON AT WHAMPOA TILL FURTHER ARRANGEMENTS ARE PRACTICIABLE AT THE NEW SETTLEMENT.

THE 'CONVENTION OF CHUENPI' WAS A TENTATIVE AGREEMENT BETWEEN ELLIOT AND QISHAN.

IT'S TERMS WERE AS FOLLOWS:

29 AUGUST 1842: THE TREATY OF NANKING IS SIGNED BETWEEN BRITAIN AND CHINA.

FORMALLY ENDING THE FIRST OPIUM WAR.
